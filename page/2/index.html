NASA--Batteries for Extreme Cold Environments

Posted on 2018-02-18 | In Research |

Requirement

  1. Expected Outcome: Proposals may focus on the development of an enabling component technology (e.g., electrolyte, anode, cathode), but must provide a feasible path to achieving the stated performance metrics based on test data and analysis.
    • Desired Performance Metrics: Operation between -40°C to -80°C
    • Achieving target values over the range of -40⁰C to -80⁰C has the greatest mission relevance; however, demonstration of operation at lower target values down to -80⁰C is still of interest
  2. Targets

    • 300 Wh/kg at C/720 rate (at cell level for primary cells) and >150 Wh/kg at C/5 rate (at cell level for rechargeable cells)
      * >200 Wh/l (at cell level for primary cells) and >150 Wh/l (at cell level for rechargeable cells)
      * Minimum 100 charge/discharge cycles with <5% loss of capacity for rechargeable cells
      * Of particular interest are cells with a nominal voltage of >2.5V for primary cells, and >3.5V for rechargeable cells

Researcher

Arumugam Manthiram

  1. Room-Temperature Aluminum-Sulfur Batteries with a Lithium-Ion-Mediated Ionic Liquid Electrolyte: Compared with lithium-sulfur (Li-S), aluminum-sulfur (Al-S) chemistry is more attractive from an economical, sustainability, and safety point of view. However, because of a lack of effective electrolytes, only limited reversibility has been realized so far with Al-S chemistry. This paper demonstrates a highly reversible room-temperature Al-S battery with a lithium-ion (Li±ion)-mediated ionic liquid electrolyte.
    • The results obtained with X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy and density functional theory calculations suggest the presence of a Li3AlS3-like product with a mixture of Li2S- and Al2S3-like phases in the discharged sulfur cathode. With Li±ion mediation, the cycle life of room-temperature Al-S batteries is greatly improved. The cell delivers an initial capacity of ∼1,000 mA hr g−1 and maintains a capacity of up to 600 mA hr g−1 after 50 cycles.

Pondermotive force

In physics, a ponderomotive force is a nonlinear force that a charged particle experiences in an inhomogeneous oscillating electromagnetic field.
$$F_P=-\frac{e^2}{4m \omega^2} \Delta (E)^2$$ which has units of newtons (in SI units) and where e is the electrical charge of the particle, m is its mass, ω is the angular frequency of oscillation of the field, and E is the amplitude of the electric field. At low enough amplitudes the magnetic field exerts very little force.

Researcher

Martin Winter

(1/29/2018 9:08:52 AM )

  1. Pre-Lithiation Strategies for Rechargeable Energy Storage Technologies: Concepts, Promises and Challenges
    • In order to compensate the loss of active lithium, mostly from the first cycle due to SEI formation, various “pre-lithiation” methods have been developed that result in an increased reversible capacity and, consequently, in a higher gravimetric energy or volumetric energy densities. This “pre-lithiation” can be conducted to both negative electrode and positive electrode. It is important to note that the degree of pre-lithiation has to be adjusted with a high accuracy. Otherwise, an over-lithiation could lead to an excess of lithium, thus lithium metal plating on top of the negative electrodes surface, resulting in safety hazards during operation.
    • In this review paper, different “Pre-lithiation” techniques for Negative electrode, positive electrode in LIB are introduced and compared. The concept of “pre-lithiation” is also extended and applied in other applications like Lithium-Ion Capacitors, Li-Ion/Sulphur Batteries, and Rechargeable Oxygen Batteries.

LIB Crucial Topic 1 - Surface coating of battery electrode (Cathode)

Posted on 2018-01-22 | In Research |

Motivation

  1. The passivating ability of the SEI layer is assumed to be due to the presence of the densely packed solid Li salt-like components at the top of graphite surface (the deepest region of SEI layer); the Li salts and solvent molecules can approach the outside of the solid Li salt-like components through the loosely packed organic layer, but only Li+ ions can cross this densely packed layer. However, a literature survey reveals that the passivating ability of SEI layers cannot be accounted for only by the solid Li salt-like components. More specifically, SEI layers generated from some SEI-forming agents such as vinylene carbonate (VC) contain dense organic layers that include poly(vinylene carbonate), which exhibit excellent passivating abilities. JES 165 (3) A575-A583 (2018)
    • Idea from this paper: Synthesize poly(vinylene carbonate) using iCVD and coat it on cathode/anode surface.
