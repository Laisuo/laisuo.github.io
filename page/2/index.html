LIB Crucial Topic 3 - LiCoO2

Posted on 2018-04-02 | In Research |

Problem of LiCoO2

  1. Although the theoretical capacity of LCO is 274 mAh/g, the practical reversible capacity is only around 140 mAh/g. Such a large irreversible capacity is mainly attributed to not only cation disorder between Li and Co, but also phase transition from hexagonal to monoclinic phase at a charge cut-off voltage ≈4.2 V, resulting from dissolution of Co into the electrolyte, and thereby generating elastic strains (> 0.1%) and micro-cracks among and within the LiCoO2 particles. This in turn responsible for internal resistance between the active material and the current collector and hence decrease in overall electrochemical performance of the cell. The structural degradation and interfacial instability between the electrode and electrolyte confined implementation of LIBs in high-end applications.
  2. To solve the structural degradation, people come up with doping techniques. To solve interfacial instability, people come up with coating methods.

LIB Crucial Topic 2 - Ni-rich cathode

Posted on 2018-03-19 | In Research |

Reviews

Manthiram: A perspective on nickel-rich layered oxide cathodes for lithium-ion batteries (2017)

Background Knowledge

Derived from LiNiO2, many LiNixMyO2 cathode materials have been developed to balance the performance of capacity, cyclability, safety, and cost. Where M stands for metal elements, like Fe, Mg, Al, Mn, Co, etc. Here are some good examples.
1. \(LiNi_{1-x}Co_xO_2\): The solid solution with cobalt in \(LiNi_{1-x}Co_xO_2\) within the entire compositional range of x from (0≤x≤1) is readily formed because of the properties similarity of Ni and Co. Importantly, compared to the pure LiNiO2 phase, cobalt substitution is beneﬁcial for reducing the cation disorder and mitigating multiple phase transitions during charge/discharge cycles. As a consequence, the Co-substituted \(LiNi_{1-x}Co_xO_2\) is able to deliver much improved electrochemical properties than the Ni-pure \(LiNiO_2\).
