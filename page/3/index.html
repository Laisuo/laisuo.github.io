Motivation

  1. The passivating ability of the SEI layer is assumed to be due to the presence of the densely packed solid Li salt-like components at the top of graphite surface (the deepest region of SEI layer); the Li salts and solvent molecules can approach the outside of the solid Li salt-like components through the loosely packed organic layer, but only Li+ ions can cross this densely packed layer. However, a literature survey reveals that the passivating ability of SEI layers cannot be accounted for only by the solid Li salt-like components. More specifically, SEI layers generated from some SEI-forming agents such as vinylene carbonate (VC) contain dense organic layers that include poly(vinylene carbonate), which exhibit excellent passivating abilities. Material Characterization Technique_XPS

Posted on 2018-01-11 | In Experiment , Research |

Principle

X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS) is based on the photoelectric effect that proposed by Einstein in 1905. When X-rays are projected to the surface of a sample, atomes in the sample are ionized and the kinetic energies of the emitted photoelectrons are measured. In this way, the binding energy of the electron can be calculated.[1]
$$E_{binding}=E_{photon}-(E_{kinetic}+\theta)$$
where $\theta$ is work function of the studied element.
Due to the small mean free path of the emitted electrons in solides (~ 10 nm), XPS is only surface sensitive. But combining with ion sputting, XPS can give depth profiles of a sample.
