LIB Crucial Topic 3 - LiCoO2

Posted on 2018-04-02 | In Research |

LiCoO2 structure

Overall intro

The rhombohedral phase is characterized by a structure with alternating layers of cobalt and lithium cations intercalated with oxygen anions. The lithium and cobalt(III) ions are arranged in intercalated layers (a). The cobalt(III) ion is located at the octahedral sites, forming a strong bond with the neighboring oxygen anion to constitute the Co−O layers (b). Finally, the lithium layers are intercalated between the CoO2 plans. The octahedral sites of these layers are occupied by lithium and cobalt(III) ions alternately that forms a sequential stacking with oxygen ion layers in a close packing of the ABCABC type (c). This specific stacking arrangement leads to an environment equivalent for all ions, allowing the maximum charge delocalization and the minimal system energy. Ref
