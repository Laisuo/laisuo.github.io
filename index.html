CVD polymerization application topic 1 Fuel Cells

Posted on 2018-03-28 | In Research |

poly(1H, 1H, 2H, 2H,-perfluorodecyl acrylate) (pPFDA)

  1. Novel hybrid fluoro-carboxylated copolymers deposited by initiated chemical vapor deposition as protonic membranes: Proton exchange membranes for fuel cells are typically made of a perﬂuorinated polymer matrix on which proton-exchange groups such as phosphonic acid (-PO3H), carboxylic acid (-COOH), or more often, sulfonic acid (-SO3H) are grafted. The main criteria required for a good conductive ionexchange membrane are a large quantity of ionizable functional groups distributed in the polymer matrix and a controlled water uptake when immersed in electrolytic solution.
  • MAA: hydrophilic and bringing the proton conductive -COOH groups, PFDA: hydrophobic.
    \[f_{MAA}=\frac{\frac{P_M}{P_{sat}}_{MAA}}{\frac{P_M}{P_{sat}}_{MAA}+\frac{P_M}{P_{sat}}_{PFDA}} \times 100%\] This gives the fraction of monomer MAA adsorbed at the surface.
    In the co-polymer, we can use information from FTIR or XPS to calculate the fraction of each co-monomer unit in the coating, indicated as \(F_{MAA}\) and \(F_{PFDA}\).
    Finemann-Ross equation can be used here to determine the monomer reactivity ratio. \[\frac{f}{F}(F-1)=r_{MAA} \frac{f^2}{F}-r_{PFDA}\] where \(f=f_{MAA}/f_{PFDA}\), \(F=F_{MAA}/F_{PFDA}\), \(r_{MAA}\) and \(r_{PFDA}\) are the reactivity ratio of MAA and PFDA, respecitively.
    Plotting the \(f(F-1)/F\) vs. \(f^2/F\), we can have the \(r_{MAA}\) from the slope and the \(r_{PFDA}\) from the intercept.
    \(r_{MAA}=0.85\) and \(r_{PFDA}=0.39\). Both r values are less than unity meaning that, when an MAA unit is terminating the polymer chain, it preferably reacts with a PFDA unit and vice versa, when a PFDA unit is terminating the polymer chain, it more readily adds an MAA monomer unit, following a moderate alternating behavior.
  1. Layered Nanostructures in Proton Conductive Polymers Obtained by Initiated Chemical Vapor Deposition: It was demonstrated that the copolymers crystallize into a bilayer structure, formed by the perﬂuorinated pendant chains of PFDA, perpendicular to the substrate surface. The MAA molecules form COOH-enriched regions among the bilayers-parallel to the substrate surface, which can act as ionic channels for proton conduction when the acid groups are deprotonated.

LIB Crucial Topic 2 - Ni-rich cathode

Posted on 2018-03-19 | In Research |

Reviews

Manthiram: A perspective on nickel-rich layered oxide cathodes for lithium-ion batteries (2017)

Background Knowledge

Derived from LiNiO2, many LiNixMyO2 cathode materials have been developed to balance the performance of capacity, cyclability, safety, and cost. Where M stands for metal elements, like Fe, Mg, Al, Mn, Co, etc. Here are some good examples.
1. \(LiNi_{1-x}Co_xO_2\): The solid solution with cobalt in \(LiNi_{1-x}Co_xO_2\) within the entire compositional range of x from (0≤x≤1) is readily formed because of the properties similarity of Ni and Co. Importantly, compared to the pure LiNiO2 phase, cobalt substitution is beneﬁcial for reducing the cation disorder and mitigating multiple phase transitions during charge/discharge cycles. As a consequence, the Co-substituted \(LiNi_{1-x}Co_xO_2\) is able to deliver much improved electrochemical properties than the Ni-pure \(LiNiO_2\).

NASA--Batteries for Extreme Cold Environments

Posted on 2018-02-18 | In Research |

Requirement

  1. Expected Outcome: Proposals may focus on the development of an enabling component technology (e.g., electrolyte, anode, cathode), but must provide a feasible path to achieving the stated performance metrics based on test data and analysis.
    • Desired Performance Metrics: Operation between -40°C to -80°C
    • Achieving target values over the range of -40⁰C to -80⁰C has the greatest mission relevance; however, demonstration of operation at lower target values down to -80⁰C is still of interest
  2. Targets

    • 300 Wh/kg at C/720 rate (at cell level for primary cells) and >150 Wh/kg at C/5 rate (at cell level for rechargeable cells)

    • 200 Wh/l (at cell level for primary cells) and >150 Wh/l (at cell level for rechargeable cells)

    • Minimum 100 charge/discharge cycles with <5% loss of capacity for rechargeable cells
    • Of particular interest are cells with a nominal voltage of >2.5V for primary cells, and >3.5V for rechargeable cells
