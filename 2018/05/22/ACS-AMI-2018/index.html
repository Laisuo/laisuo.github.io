ACS AMI - 2018

Posted on 2018-05-22 | In Journal | |

Volume 10

Issue 19

  1. Dissolution Mechanisms of LiNi1/3Mn1/3Co1/3O2 Positive Electrode Material from Lithium-Ion Batteries in Acid Solution: Currently, some synthesis methods of new cathode materials and a large majority of recycling processes are based on the use of acidic solutions. This study addresses the mechanistic and limiting aspects on the dissolution of the layered LiNi1/3Mn1/3Co1/3O2 oxide in acidic solution. The dissolution of the active cathode material in two steps, as shown below.
    • Step 1: “self-regulating” by the lithium deintercalation and the charge compensation of TMs and partially by the oxygen reaction. The delithiation process leads to a positie potential shift of particles, which reduces the leaching driving force and limits the dissolution.
    • Step 2: “Surface reorganization” from layered strcture to a metastable birnessite phase and a transformation in the \(\gamma\)-type manganese oxide.

  1. Synergistic Effect between LiNi0.5Co0.2Mn0.3O2 and LiFe0.15Mn0.85PO4/C on Rate and Thermal Performance for Lithium Ion Batteries: It is found that the outstanding rate and thermal performance of the blend cathode is due to the prominent synergistic effect between NCM523 and LFMP/C, and 10% LFMP/C in the blend cathode materials is the most adaptable as considering both electrochemical and thermal properties simultaneously.

  2. N‑Allyl‑N,N‑Bis(trimethylsilyl)amine as a Novel Electrolyte Additive To Enhance the Interfacial Stability of a Ni-Rich Electrode for LithiumIon Batteries: Electrolyte oxidation reactions and electrode structural destruction are greatly suppressed in the electrolyte with NNB additive, leading to improved cyclic stability of LNCA from 72.8 to 86.2% after 300 cycles.

Issue 16

  1. Synthesis and Electrochemical Properties of LiNi0.5Mn1.5O4 for Li-Ion Batteries by the Metal−Organic Framework Method: Spinel LiNi0.5Mn1.5O4 was synthesized using a novel one-step calcining method based on MOFs. It shows excellent specific capacity of 145 mAh g−1 at a discharge rate of 0.1C and 105 mAh g−1 at a discharge rate of 20C. Even over 1000 cycles at 5C, the capacity retention was 86.6%.
