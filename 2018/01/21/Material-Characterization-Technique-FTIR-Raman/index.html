Material Characterization Technique - FTIR & Raman

Posted on 2018-01-21 | In Experiment , Research | |

Principle

FTIR

Wiki:Fourier-transform infrared spectroscopy (FTIR) is a technique used to obtain an infrared spectrum of absorption or emission of a solid, liquid or gas. It can measure how well a sample absorbs light at each wavelength.
Youtube Vedio: (1)Different functional groups bend, stretch and wag at different frequencies. (2) A functional group will absorb light if the frequency of the light matches the frequency of stretching, bending, or wagging. (3) IR spectra show absorption bands that enable to determine if certain functional groups are present in a molecule.

Raman

Wiki: Raman spectroscopy is commonly used in chemistry to provide a structural fingerprint by which molecules can be identified. It relies on inelastic scattering of monochromatic light, as shown below. The laser light interacts with molecular vibrations, phonons or other excitations in the system, resulting in the energy of the laser photons being shifted up or down. The shift in energy gives information about the vibrational modes in the system.
Spontaneous Raman scattering is typically very weak, and as a result the main difficulty of Raman spectroscopy is separating the weak inelastically scattered light from the intense Rayleigh scattered laser light.
\[\Delta w=(\frac{1}{\lambda_0}-\frac{1}{\lambda_1})\]
where \(\Delta w\) is the Raman shift expressed in wavenumber[\(cm^{-1}\)], \(\lambda_0\) is the excitation wavelength, and \(\lambda_1\) is the Raman spectrum wavelength.

Comparison between FTIR and Raman

1.The main difference between the two techniques lies in the nature of the molecular transitions taking place.[1]
* Since the Raman utilize inelastic scattering of light, a transition to be Raman active there must be a change in the polarizability of the molecule during the vibration.
Think in this way: If you throw a ball to a wall, the change of the ball speed largely depends on the hardness of the wall. The speed does not change if the wall is very hard (elastic reflection), while the speed changes a lot if the wall is very soft (inelastic reflection). The idea of Raman is the same, if the polarizability of the molecule vabration does not change (hard wall), then it is inactive to the Raman signal. Therefore, a molecule vibratin with big change in the polarizability is required to be Raman active
* On the other hand, for an IR detectable transition, the molecule must undergo dipole moment change during vibration. Thus, symmetric molecules like O2 does not have any IR observation line since the molecules cannot change its dipole moment.

2.Regarding the excitation wavelength, Raman uses monochromatic beam or laser, while IR utilizes light with a range of wavelength.

3.Finally, the Raman technique requires high-stability laser sources and sensitive amplification equipment to detect the weak signal.

MUTUAL EXCLUSION PRINCIPLE: In a molecule with a center of symmetry it is seen that vibrations that are Raman active are IR inactive and vice-versa, this is called the Principle of mutual exclusion. In molecules with different elements of symmetry, certain bands may be active in IR, Raman, both or neither. For a complex molecule that has no symmetry except identity element, all of the normal modes are active in both IR and Raman. In general the strong bands in the IR spectrum of a compound corresponds to weak bands in the Raman and vice versa. Mutual exclusion principle as seen in \(CO_2\).

Despite their differences, both techniques serve the purpose of identifying unknown substances. Molecules that cannot be detected with the one method can be easily detected with the other, and that leads researchers to use them both in a complementary way.

Advantages and Disadvantages

  • The key advantage of Raman spectroscopy is that it requires little to no sample preparation, while FITR method has constraints on sample thickness, uniformity and dilution to avoid saturation.[2]
