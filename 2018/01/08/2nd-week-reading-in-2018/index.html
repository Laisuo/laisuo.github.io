Subject: Energy science and technology

  1. Molecular understanding of polyelectrolyte binders that actively regulate ion transport in sulfur cathodes. (December 22, 2017)

Replacing PVDF binder by PEB-1 binder, the performance of Li-S cell is largely improved. To understand this improvement, the fundermental effect of binder is further studied and discussed.
Basically, the chemical constitution of a polymer binder should dictate wheter it is passive, active, or adaptive in in an electrode. But currently, it is not clear in literature, which casue obscure to rational design a polymer binder.
In this work, it shows polyelectrolyte binders actively exert their influence on the diffusive transport of various ionic species encountered in the cycling of sulfur electrodes. The effect includes both the facilitated transport of Li+ throughout the electrode and restricted active material diffusion so as to minimizing capacity fade at high sulfur loading.
More details of their discovery can be found in the final Discussion part of the article.

Authours Update

Martin Winter

  1. JES, Thermal Analysis of LiNi0. 4Co0. 2Mn0. 4O2/Mesocarbon Microbeads Cells and Electrodes: State-of-Charge and State-of-Health Influences on Reaction Kinetics
    The thermal stability of 5 Ah pouch cells (NMC422/MCMB) is studied here. Cathode electrode and anode electrode were obtained at differet state of charge and state of health by disassembling, and their thermal stabilities were tested seperately. The general conclusion is that (1) The self heating onset temperature decrease with aging, and with incrasing of SOC, (2) Anode is more stable than cathode.
    However, the conclusion here cannot be applied directly to full cell becasue the case is much more complex in full cell, including the effect of SEI, separator, and cathode-anode interaction. Thus, more studies need to be done to uncover the thermal stability of the cell.
  2. JPS, New insights into pre-lithiation kinetics of graphite anodes via nuclear magnetic resonance spectroscopy
    This article for the first time utilized NMR to monitor the apparent rate(s) of lithium metal dissolution and formation of the corresponding LiCx phases during the pre-lithiation process, yielding insights into apparent reaction kinetics of the pre-lithiation process. The dissolution rate of lithium metal decreases with the increasing of pre-lithiation degree.
