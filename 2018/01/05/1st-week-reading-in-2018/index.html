1st week reading in 2018

Posted on 2018-01-05 | In Reading

Science News

  1. What’s coming up in 2018 Link

    The migration of scientists is not clear now due to the complex environment in U.S., European Union, and China. Here are some views about the development in several important aspects in the upcoming year.

    Astronomy: A monster Black Hole that detected by by the Event Horizon Telescope team in April 2017 at the heart of our galaxy. After processing and correlated the data, they will give some interesting information.
    Human origins: New cleus from ancient DNA. Besides obtained from bones, scientists announced that they had managed to pick it up from cave sediments, which will largely help scientists understand the evolution of humanbeings.
    Public health: Epidemics make a return
    Computer science: Quantum computing might coming soon with the hardworking of physicists in the last decades. The quantum computer relies on subtle phenomena like interference of quantum waves to perform calculations rather than traditional flipping ordinary bits, which gives it overwhelming power.
    Biomedicine: Based on project All of Us study proposed by then-President Barack Obama in 2015, the U.S. National Institues of Health will hit the launch button on its most ambitious project ever that will take 10 years and cost $ 4 billion.
    Biotechnology: A court ruling on gene editing. Public opposition to genetically modified organisms (GMOs), even though it does not insert foreign DNA from viruses or bacteria to alter a plant’s function.
    Policy: The UK decision to leave European Union in 2019 has raised so many unanswered questions, and will still be policy variables in the upcoming year.
    Funding: The European Commission’s next ambitious funding program for research and innovation will start taking shape this year. It is a 7-year program that follows Horizon 2020, which provide €77 billion between 2014 and 2020. However, the leaving of U.K. leads to a hole of €10 billion or €11 billion to the project.
    Clinical research: RNA treatments move forward. The treatment using RNA is tricky becasue along with these delicate RNA payloads past the body’s defenses and into the desired tissue, there are some toxic effects from their delivery vehicles.
    Public Health: Shots may treat HIV with an effect of 1-3 month, which takes much longer than pills that needs to be taken daily.
    Space flight: China and India are planning to put down landers on the moon during the year (the Chang’e 4 missions and Chandrayaan-2 missions) and five companies are vying to deposit rovers there before the end of March to claim the Google Lunar X-Prize. Donald Trump also directed NASA to return U.S. astronauts to the moon, but it might be a little bit late.
    Particle physics: Physicists are still in pursuit of new particles, although the last missing piece in the standard model of particles and forces, Higgs boson, has already been found in 2012.
    Drug oversight: Food and Drug Administration (FDA) restricts stem cell therapies.
    Politics: Many U.S. researchers have decided to take their activism to the next level by running for office.
