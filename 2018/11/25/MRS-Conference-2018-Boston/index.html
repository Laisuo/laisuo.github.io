Nov. 25

Symposium ET06

Topic: The history of Li- and Li-ion Batteries

Martine Winter (1:30pm - 3:00 pm)

Topic: Lithium Based Anodes: A look back into the history

  • The further back you can look, the further forward you are likely to see.
  1. Many Battery materials have been proposed, but only very few succeed
    1. In 2017 market, lead acid batteries still dominate in the aspect of MWh. However, the LIB and lead acid are comparable in the aspect of cost.
  2. Why Li?
    1. Periodic table
    2. First Li discovery: 1818 by Johan August Arfwedson.
    3. 1958, EC and PC are reported -> good electrolyte enables batteries
    4. 1960s, Space and military use motivate Li batteries
    5. 1970s and 1980s, failed to commercalized LIBs
      1. 1970s. Li/TiS2, LiAl/TiS2 rechargable battery (EXXON)
      2. 1980s. Li/MoS2 (Moli Energy)
  3. Why Li-ion
    1. Li is unsafe, as reported in 1989. Many news about Moli Energy.
  4. Strength and Weakness
    1. Common knowledge: there are rechargable batteries with higher capacity than LIB, but always lower specific energy. (eg. Ni-MH, AA cell)
  5. Anode Before 1991
    1. Book “A nameless battery with untold stories” by Isao Kuribayashi
    2. Metal -> alloy, Graphte -> intercalation
    3. Graphite. PC, EC
  6. Comparison of different anodes
    1. C, Sn, Si, Ge
  7. Summary
    1. It takes 30 years to commercialize a battery
    2. There will be several similar technologies coexistance, not one totally substitude another.

Kang Xu

Topic: Electrolytes and interphases in LIBs

  1. The Dawn of LIB
    1. early understanding of SEI
    2. Aurbach’s contribution on understanding SEI: 50% organic componts
  2. The intercalation bypass
    1. Host-guest chemistry (1987 Nobel prize)
    2. Dual intercalation battery
    3. Cathode development: 1976, TiS2; 1980, LiCoO2 (Oxides are more stable than sulfides); 1983, LiMn2O4…
    4. Intercalation vs. insertion: LiCoO2 vs. LiFePO4
  3. The anode challenge
    1. Graphite is an ideal host
    2. Early patents on LiC6
      1. 1979 Bell lab (Basc): Molten salt
      2. 1981 Sanyo (Ikeda): PC/ ether electrolyte
      3. 1983 CNRS (Yazami): LiClO4 in PEO electrolyte. (This is the first time that LiC6 is formed correctly)
  4. The birth of LIB
    1. 1983, the 1st demo by Akira Yoshina
    2. 1986
    3. 1990, Sony (Nishi)
  5. The Graphite challenge (the 3rd scenario)
    1. EC-containing; PC-free (<10%); Ether-free
    2. Why EC is not used in the beginning? –> EC and PC have the same properties, people chose PC becasue of its low melting point. They paid the price by ignoring EC for more than 2 decades.
    3. three laws of SEI. 1) products from electrolyte reduction; 2) Li+ conductor; 3) electron insulation
  6. Battery and interphase fundermentals
    1. Most challenging because it working with non-equilibrium
    2. interface matters
  7. Beyond LIBs
    1. Solvation in aqueous and non-aqueous electrolytes
    2. stabilized water (>3 V)

Nov. 26 (Monday)

CM03

8:15 AM *CM03.01.01
Topic: Single Particles Cathode Material Electrochemical Properties from Micro-Pipets Methods (Canada)
- Scanning Micropipettle contact method (SMCM): This method can test isolated LFP active particles!

9:00 AM CM03.01.03

Topic: Enabling Ultrafast Interfacial Li-Transport in Layered-Oxide Cathodes (Bohua Wen, Yet-Ming Chiang’s group, MIT)
- From MD simulation, it seems the 1st shell structure may be responcible for the rate limitation.
- Calculated exchange current density (j0) from both EIS and PITT methods.

ET01

9:30 AM *ET01.01.06 Topic: Sussing out Important Parameters for Cathode Coatings in All-Solid-State Batteries (Lincoln Miara, Samsung)
- The talk focuses on Charge-transfer resistance in solid state battery
- Solid electrolytes are often unstable against both Cathode and anode (intrinsic stability of many SSE)
- Use coating to improve stability. eg. LZO coating can stabilize voltage up to 4 V. (thermodynamically 3.2 V, but kinetically 4.0 V)
- Simulation to choose coatings by applying electrochemical filter and chemical filter.

10:30 AM *ET01.01.07
Topic: Nanoscale Composite Polymer Electrolyte Batteries (Yi Cui)
1. Comparison of two types of solide electrolytes 1. Ceramic electrolyte: (1) high ionic conductivity (10^-3 S/cm), (2) poor processing, (3) interfacial and chemical stability issues
2. Polymer electrolyte: (1) easy processing, (2) low ionic conductivity (< 10^-5 S/cm), (3) low mechanical strength.
2. Ionic conductivity of polymer with ceramic nanowire fillers 1. LLTO nanowire by electrospinning. 15% LLTO nanowire in PAN, LiClO4 give best conductivity (Wei Liu, Yi Cui, 2015, Nano Letters, 15, 2740)
2. LLTO lewis acid bond clO4-, release Li+ 3. Aligning ceramic nanowires (Wei Liu, Yi Cui, Nature ENergy 2, 17035 2017): The calculated interfacial ionic conductivity reaches 10^-2 S/cm at 30°C
3. Mechanical strength: SiO2 aerogel network with PEO electrolyte filling 1. Vertically alligned inorganic composite polymer electrolyte in AAO temperlate
4. Flowable interface: Li metal electrode issue (Science Advance, 3, 0713, 2017)

11:00 AM ET01.01.08

Topic: Fabrication of Advanced Composite Cathodes in All-Solid-State Batteries Using 3D Reconstruction Analysis and Complementary Wet-Based Process (Sungjun Choi)

11:30 AM ET01.01.10

Phase-Field Simulations of Lithium Dendrite Growth with Open-Source Software (Zijian Hong, Venkat, CMU)
- Lithium dendrite growth (Simulation) - Phase field software

ET06

1:30 PM *ET06.02.01
Topic: All-Solid-State Lithium Metal Batteries Utilizing Polyrotaxanes as New Family of Solid Polymer Electrolytes (Martin Winter)

2:15 PM ET06.02.03

Topic: Stabilizing Polymer Electrolytes in High-Voltage Lithium Batteries (Snehashis Choudhury, Cornell)
- Most polymer electrolytes rely on ether based chemistries for ion transport, which degrade at > 4.1 V
- Solution: artifical SEI

3:00 PM *ET06.02.04
Topic: Design Principles for Solid Electrolyte–Electrode Interfaces in All-Solid-State Li-Ion Batteries (Yifei Mo)
- Han Zhu. Adv Energy Mate. 2016, 1501590
- Li/SE interface is very resistive
- LiPON has compatible interface with Li (form Li3P, Li3N, Li2O)

4:00 PM ET06.02.06

Topic: Effects of Polymer Coatings on Electrodeposited Lithium Metal (Jeffrey F. Lopez, Cui Yi’s group)
- “G. Zhang, Cui Yi, ACS Energy Letter 2016”. Since then, there are lots of work in this field.
- How to design a good polymer as the coating? (1) Mechanical property affects globle growth, while chemcial affects local growth. (2) Surface energy and dielect constant affect Li deposite. - (J. Lopez, A. Pei, Y. Cui. Z Bao et al. JACS 2018) - Here are some factors: Thickness, mechanics, surface energy/dielectric constant, reactivity.

8:15 AM ET01.03.01

Topic: The Structure and Conductivity of 3D Superionic Na3SbS4 Conductor at Extremely Low Temperatures (Hui Wang)
- From element table –> Na3SbS4 is chosen, which has conductivity of 1 ~ 3 mS/cm, and it is stable in air (Chemically).

Topic: Electrochemomechanical Failure in Active Materials and Solid Electrolytes (Yet-Ming Chiang, MIT)

- Critical overpotencial and critical current of LLZO solid electrolyte. - Single crystal LLZTO with/without defects
- Lithium metal penetration prefer through the edge of SE

Topic: Interface Engineering of the Garnet-Based Solid-State Batteries and In Situ Neutron Depth Profiling Diagnose (Chengwei Wang)

2:00 PM *ET01.04.03 Topic: Advanced Sulfide Solid Electrolyte by Core-Shell Structural Design—Theory and Experiment (Xin Li; Harvard University) Advanced sulfide solid electrolyte by core-shellstructural design - Voltage stability is a issue for sulfide solide electrolyte. Li-Si-P-S (1.7 - 2.1 V)

2:30 pm

Topic: Electrochemical Characterization of All-Solid-State Battery with Li7La3Zr2O12 Solid Electrolyte Kiyoshi Kanamura; Tokyo Metropolitan University
- The interface between Li metal anode and LLZO solid electrolyte pellet is large due to poor contact. The contact has been improved by using an interlayer between Li metal and LLZO. Au film between Li chip and LLZO. Without such layer, coin cell cannot be charged/discharged. (Creat good contact between LLZO and Li)

3:30 PM

Topic: Construction of Flexible Interfacial Layer for Solid-State Li Batteries (Yong Yang; Xiamen Univ.)
- Use pencil to draw a few layers of carbon on LLZO -> increase the wettability of LLZO to Li

4:15 PM

Topic: Tuning Charge Diffusion in Polyethylene Oxide-Based Electrolytes Using Secondary Sites—Insights from Molecular Dynamics Simulations (Arthur France-Lanord, MIT)

4:45 PM

Topic: Fabrication of Ultrathin Films of Li7-3xAlxLa3Zr2O12 with Tunable Surface Morphology by Spray Pyrolysis (Zachary D. Hood, MIT)
- Thin film Li Garnet electrolyte - >1000 C for densification of grains in a tape/pellet
