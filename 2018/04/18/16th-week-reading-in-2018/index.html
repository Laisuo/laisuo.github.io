Research

Martin Winter

  1. Nature Energy Performance and cost of materials for lithium-based rechargeable automotive batteries: This Review surveys the state-of-the-art and recent advances in active materials, electrolytes, separators and cell chemistries for automotive batteries. We focus on three most important aspects: performance, production and cost. We also give a perspective on future developments at the end.
  • Anode material: Artificial graphites (SGs) and natural graphites (NGs) as well as amorphous (hard and soft) carbons, can be considered as state-of-the-art negative electrode materials. The current market share of 43% of SG and 46% of NG (2016 data) for LIBs, with only 7% for amorphous carbon, clearly evidences the dominance of carbon-based anode materials.
  • Cathode material: Since the commercialization of LIBs, the cathode has proven a bottleneck with regard to specific capacities. Layered oxides with mixed transition metal composition are typically prepared over a co-precipitation route. An aqueous solution of the transition metal salts (for example, MSO4) and a base (NaOH or Na2CO3) as well as a surfactant (NH4OH) are pumped into a continuously stirred tank reactor (CSTR), in which the metal hydroxide or carbonate precipitates. After filtering and repeatedly washing to remove impurities, the material is dried and sieved before it is mixed with a stoichiometric amount of a lithium salt. The desired product is formed through calcination at 650–950 °C.
  • Electrolytes and separators: Recent studies verified, contrary to common belief in literature, that these state-of-the-art electrolytes are oxidatively quite stable up to 5.0 V or higher, versus Li/Li+ at 20 °C, and thus can be used in combination with high voltage cathode materials.

ECS

  1. Carbon Nanotubes as Effective Interlayer for High Performance Li-I2 Batteries: Long Cycle Life and Superior Rate Performance: Among various candidates, rechargeable lithium-iodine batteries are increasingly attracting attention, due to its high theoretical capacity (LiI: 211 mAh g−1), low cost, and potential super rate performance owing to rapid electrochemical reversibility of I2/I− redox pair. However, iodine and its discharged products (I2, I− and I3− ions) are highly soluble in most polar and nonpolar solvents. Herein, a compacted carbon nanotubes film is inserted between I2 cathode and separator as the iodine species prohibitor.

  2. Size Dependence of Transport Non-Uniformities on Localized Plating in Lithium-Ion Batteries: we probe the importance of the size scale and geometry on localized plating through numerical simulations and experiments. Using modified separators to create transport non-uniformities, we show that certain geometric features lead to more vulnerability to plating, and localization strongly depends on size. A single large feature in a separator induces more plating than a collection of smaller features with same total area.
