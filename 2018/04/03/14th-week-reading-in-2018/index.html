Researcher

Martin Winter

  1. Triphenylphosphine Oxide as Highly Effective Electrolyte Additive for Graphite/NMC811 Lithium Ion Cells: In this work, we introduce triphenylphosphine oxide (TPPO) as electrolyte additive for the application in graphite/LiNi0.8Mn0.1Co0.1O2 (NMC811) cells. The addition of only 0.5 wt.% TPPO into a carbonate-based electrolyte (LiPF6 in EC:EMC) significantly increases the 1st cycle Coulombic efficiency (CE) as well as the reversible specific capacity and improves the capacity retention of the LIB full cell cycled between 2.8 - 4.3 V.
  1. Synthesis, electrochemical investigation and structural analysis of doped Li [Ni0.6Mn0.2Co0.2-xMx]O2 (x = 0, 0.05; M = Al, Fe, Sn) cathode materials: All synthesized materials show a well-defined layered structure of the hexagonal α-NaFeO2 phase investigated by X-ray diffraction (XRD). Undoped LiNi0.6Mn0.2Co0.2O2 exhibits a discharge capacity of 170 mAh/g in Li-metal 2032 coin-type cells. Doped materials reach lower capacities between 145 mAh/g for Al and 160 mAh/g for Sn. However, all doped materials prolong the cycle life by up to 20%. Besides, the thermal stability is improved by the doping of Sn, which is proven by thermal investigations with the help of Differential Scanning Calorimetry (DSC) and Thermogravimetric Analysis (TGA).

Arumugam Manthiram

  1. Stable surface chemistry against ambient air of modified high-nickel cathodes for lithium-ion batteries: In this study, we demonstrate that substituting a small amount of Al for Ni in the crystal lattice notably improves the chemical stability against air by limiting the formation of LiOH, Li2CO3, LiHCO3, and NiO in the near-surface region.
    • Al-doping notably suppresses the unfavorable chemical reaction between high-Ni oxides and moisture/carbon dioxide in air via a robust lattice structure facilitated by the strong Al-O covalent bonds. As a result, the Al-doped 9406 sample shows less degradation (such as Li2CO3 and NiO) on the secondary particle surface relative to the dopant-free 9406 sample.

Surface-protected LiCoO2 with ultrathin solid oxide electrolyte film for high-voltage lithium ion batteries and lithium polymer batteries: Surface modification of LiCoO2 with the ultrathin film of solid state electrolyte of Li1.4Al0.4Ti1.6(PO4)3 (LATP) has been realized by a new and facile solution-based method. The coated LiCoO2 reveals enhanced structural and electrochemical stability at high voltage (4.5 V vs Li+/Li) in half-cell with liquid electrolyte.

A four-eyed lizard walked the earth 49 million years ago: If you lived in what is now Wyoming 49 million years ago, you could have spotted a four-eyed lizard—the one and only known example of such a creature among jawed vertebrates. They had two standard eyes and also sported so-called pineal and parapineal “eyes” on the top of its head. Many vertebrates alive today—such as some turtles, lizards, and fish—have a third “eye” on the top of their head, which may be important for sensing direction or regulating the animal’s biological clock.
